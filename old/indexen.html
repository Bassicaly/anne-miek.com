WHAT IS OSTEOPATHY


Osteopathy is a therapy which originated in America and makes it's way through England, France and Belgium to the Netherlands. The 7 years of training is only allowed for physiotherapists and/or medical doctors. Founder of the osteopathy is Dr. A. Still (1828-1917). He discovered that any physical complaint is caused by a limitation of movement in one or more tissues of the human body:
  • joints, muscles, tendons/fascia and bones (parietal)
  • viscera, nerves, glandula, blood- and lymph system (visceral)
  • structures of the cranium (cranial)
All of these parts are connected with one another and will influence each other in a positive or negative way. A loss of mobility will cause less circulatory flow through the tissues and the consequences that go with it. Besides that, the body will compensate this loss of mobility at another part, what can cause to much strain on the tissues. In this way you create problem on problem and you can end up in a vicious circle. Therefore the key of an osteopathic treatment is a thoroughly built reasoning through extensive knowledge of the human physiology, anatomy, biomechanics, etc.
Osteopathy is a complete way of treatment; it not only looks into the symptoms or the complaint, but tries to find out where the problem is coming from.
  • What is the real cause?
  • What other parts of the body are involved?
  • Which hidden problems or illnesses are connected?


Some examples;

- When you have (slight) chronic backache you can change your way of walking and sprain your ankle more easily.
- Headaches can be caused by head trauma, but also through tension in the head region or by problems of the neck, spine, pelvic area or even from organs.
- Backache can originate from a slipped disc. However, each part of the spine is also connected with our organs and can influence each other. Think e.g. of lower backache and problems with the womb.

Treatment


After an extensive questionnaire the osteopath will examine the entire body of the patient in search of lack of mobility. There are a number of techniques to relax the tissues involved. The osteopath will only be using his/her hands. The most common are:

Manipulation

The purpose is to release joint blockage or improve mobility in joint(s). Usually it's a quick and painless action.

Organ- or visceral techniques

By moving the organs and their surrounding tissues the circulation improves. Possible adhesions, for instance after surgery or when the womb is prolapsed, can be released.

Head- or cranial techniques

The skull (cranium) exists off numeral bones which will keep moving. These bones are connected with each other through sutures. If this was not the case, every fall on the head would result in a skull fracture. Attached on these sutures start membranes of connective tissue (fasciae), which run to all the body. They surround our brain, the spinal cord, the organs and all the muscles, nerves and blood vessels. This way an osteopath can, by working on the head, influence other parts of our body.
